Scheme for Financial Assistance for Veteran Artists

Overview

The “Scheme for Financial Assistance for Veteran Artists”, formerly known as the “Scheme for Pension and Medical Aid to Artists”, is implemented by the Ministry of Culture. It aims to improve the socio-economic status of aged artists and scholars who have contributed significantly in the fields of art and culture, and now live in penury or poor conditions.

Benefits

  • Monthly allowance of ₹6,000/- per eligible beneficiary.
  • In the event of a beneficiary’s death, assistance may be transferred to the spouse (upon request within 1 year).
  • State Govt. must contribute a minimum of ₹500/month. Central Govt. provides the remainder up to ₹6000.
  • If the State offers no pension, full ₹6000 is provided by the Central Govt.

Eligibility

  • Minimum age of 60 years (except spouse cases).
  • Income (including spouse's) must not exceed ₹4,000/month or ₹48,000/year.
  • Significant contribution in arts, letters, or traditional knowledge.
  • Receiving State pension (minimum ₹500) or recommended by Zonal Cultural Centres.
  • Not receiving financial aid under other Ministry of Culture schemes (e.g. Repertory Grant).

Application Process

Modes: Online / Offline

  1. Apply online via Culture Scheme Monitoring System (CSMS) at Ministry of Culture website.
  2. Applications are reviewed by an Expert Committee after field verification.
  3. Approved names are submitted for final approval by the Competent Authority.
  4. On approval, funds are disbursed via PFMS/DBT or designated Service Provider to the applicant’s bank account.

Required Documents

  • Recent passport-size photo (within 6 months).
  • Address proof (Aadhaar, voter ID, utility bill, etc.).
  • Proof of Date of Birth (Aadhaar, PAN, passport, etc.).
  • Self-attested copy of Aadhaar card.
  • Original Income Certificate (Annexure-II(A)) issued by competent authority.
  • Documents proving contributions in art, letters, awards, recognitions, etc.
  • Bank Authorization Letter (Annexure-IV) verified by Bank Manager.
  • Recommendation from the State/UT Culture Department in prescribed format (Appendix).

For Pension Transfer to Spouse (After Death of Artist)

  • Application within 1 year of artist’s death.
  • Death certificate of the artist (original/self-attested).
  • Proof of artist’s pension receipt.
  • Legal heir certificate & affidavit (from SDM or 1st Class Magistrate).
  • Digital Life Certificate (DLC) of spouse via Jeevan Pramaan Portal.
  • Latest Income Certificate of spouse (Annexure-II(A)).
  • Spouse’s Aadhaar and address proof.
  • Undertaking stating no financial assistance from other schemes.
  • Bank Authorization Letter (Annexure-IV).

Renewal & Life Certificate

  • Submit Digital Life Certificate (DLC) annually in November via Jeevan Pramaan Portal.
  • Submit Income Certificate every 5 years.
  • Failure to submit required documents may result in stoppage of pension.

Discontinuation

  • Pension is discontinued if income exceeds ₹4,000/month or ₹48,000/year (excluding artist pension).
  • The government reserves the right to terminate pension after giving 3-month notice.
  • Beneficiaries may voluntarily relinquish pension via written notice.
