Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana

Women & Child

Details

In May 2016, the Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas (MoPNG) introduced the Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY) as a flagship scheme to make clean cooking fuel such as LPG available to rural and deprived households which were otherwise using traditional cooking fuels like firewood, coal and cow-dung cakes. The use of traditional fuels had a harmful impact on the health of rural women as well as on the environment.

The scheme was launched on 1st May 2016 in Ballia, Uttar Pradesh by the Hon’ble Prime Minister of India, Shri Narendra Modi.

  • The target was to release 8 crore LPG connections to deprived households by March 2020.
  • On 7th September 2019, the 8th crore LPG connection was handed over in Aurangabad, Maharashtra.
  • LPG coverage increased from 62% on 1st May 2016 to 99.8% as on 1st April 2021.
  • Under Union Budget FY 2021–22, provision was made to release an additional 1 crore LPG connections under PMUY with special facilities for migrant families.

Benefits

  • ₹1600 for a 14.2 kg cylinder connection or ₹1150 for a 5 kg cylinder connection.
  • All PMUY beneficiaries will be provided with the first LPG refill and stove (hot plate) free of cost along with their deposit-free connection.

Note: Priority is given to states with lower LPG coverage compared to the national average as on 1st January 2016.

Eligibility

An adult woman belonging to a poor household and not having an LPG connection in her household will be eligible under Ujjwala 2.0. Beneficiaries should belong to any of the following categories:

  • Eligible as per SECC 2011 list.
  • Belonging to SC/ST households, PMAY beneficiaries, Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) beneficiaries, forest dwellers, Most Backward Classes (MBC), Tea and Ex-Tea Garden Tribes, and people residing in river islands (with supporting documents).
  • If not covered under the above categories, the applicant may apply under poor household category by submitting a 14-point declaration (as per format).

Exclusions

  • Male members of a household are not eligible to apply for this scheme.

Documents Required

  1. Valid photo ID.
  2. Ration card issued by the State / other State Government document certifying family composition / Self-Declaration as per Annexure I (for migrant applicants).
  3. Proof of Address – Aadhaar can be used as both identity and address proof if the connection is required at the same address.
  4. Bank account number and IFSC code.
  5. Aadhaar (not mandatory for Assam and Meghalaya).
  6. Passport size photograph.
