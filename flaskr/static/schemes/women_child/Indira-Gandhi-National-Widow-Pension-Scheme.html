Indira Gandhi National Widow Pension Scheme (IGNWPS)

Women & Child

Details

The Indira Gandhi National Widow Pension Scheme (IGNWPS) is implemented by the Ministry of Rural Development, Government of India. It is a non-contributory pension scheme aimed at providing social security to widows belonging to poor families living below the poverty line (BPL).

Benefits

  • A pension of ₹300 per month is provided to widows between 40 and 79 years of age.
  • For widows aged 80 years and above, a pension of ₹500 per month is provided.

Eligibility

  • The applicant must be a widow in the age group of 40 to 79 years.
  • The applicant should belong to a household living below the poverty line (BPL) as per the criteria prescribed by the Government of India.

Exclusions

  • Pension will be discontinued in case of remarriage of the widow.
  • Pension will also be discontinued once the widow moves above the poverty line.

Documents Required

  • Death certificate of the husband with the name of the surviving wife (widow).
  • BPL card.
  • Age proof – Birth certificate or school certificate. In their absence, ration card or EPIC may be considered. If no valid document is available, a Medical Officer of any government hospital may issue an age certificate.
