Mahila Samman Savings Certificate

Overview

The Mahila Samman Savings Certificate (MSSC) was launched by the Department of Economic Affairs, Ministry of Finance, to provide financial security to every girl and woman in India. The scheme has been operational since April 1, 2023 and is valid for a two-year period up to March 31, 2025. It is available at Post Offices and all eligible Scheduled Public and Private Sector Banks.

Key Features

  • Attractive and secured investment option for all girls and women.
  • Account can be opened on or before March 31, 2025 for a tenure of two years.
  • Interest rate of 7.5% per annum, compounded quarterly.
  • Minimum deposit: ₹1,000 (in multiples of ₹100); Maximum deposit: ₹2,00,000.
  • Partial withdrawal of up to 40% of the eligible balance after 1 year.
  • Multiple accounts allowed with a 3-month gap between openings, subject to ₹2 lakh total limit.

Benefits

  • Safe and fixed return investment for girls and women.
  • Flexible deposit and withdrawal options.
  • 7.5% interest compounded quarterly and credited to the account.
  • Two-year maturity period from the date of deposit.

Eligibility

  • Must be an Indian citizen.
  • Only women and girl children are eligible.
  • Minor accounts can be opened by a guardian.
  • No upper age limit; open to all women.
  • Only single-holder accounts are allowed.

Deposits

  • Multiple accounts allowed with a 3-month gap between openings.
  • Deposit must be between ₹1,000 and ₹2,00,000, in multiples of ₹100.
  • No additional deposits are allowed after opening an account.

Payment on Maturity

  • Account matures after 2 years from the date of opening.
  • Eligible balance is paid on maturity.
  • Amounts are rounded off to the nearest rupee.

Withdrawal Rules

  • Up to 40% of the eligible balance can be withdrawn after 1 year but before maturity.
  • Guardian can withdraw from minor accounts by providing required certificates.
  • Withdrawals are rounded off to the nearest rupee.

Premature Closure

  1. Allowed in cases of:
    • Death of account holder.
    • Extreme compassionate grounds (e.g., medical emergency, death of guardian).
  2. Interest paid as per the scheme rate for the period held.
  3. For closures after 6 months (for reasons other than above), interest is reduced by 2%.
  4. Amounts are rounded off to the nearest rupee.

Application Process (Offline)

  1. Visit the nearest Post Office or designated bank.
  2. Collect or download the application form.
  3. Fill out the form and attach required documents.
  4. Fill in declaration and nomination details.
  5. Submit the form with the deposit amount.
  6. Receive the certificate as proof of investment.

Note: Account must be opened on or before March 31, 2025.

Documents Required

  • Passport size photograph
  • Birth Certificate (for age proof)
  • Aadhaar Card
  • PAN Card
  • Pay-in slip or cheque for deposit
  • Valid ID and Address proof (any one of the following):
    • Passport
    • Driving License
    • Voter ID
    • Job card issued by NREGA signed by a State Govt. officer
    • Letter issued by the National Population Register
