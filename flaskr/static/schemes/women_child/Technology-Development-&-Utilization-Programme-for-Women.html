Technology Development & Utilization Programme for Women (TDUPW)

Women & Child

Details

The Technology Development & Utilization Programme for Women (TDUPW) has been formulated by the Department of Scientific and Industrial Research (DSIR) to meet the specific needs of women and to enhance their contribution towards technology capability building. DSIR works towards indigenous technology promotion, development, utilization and transfer.

Under the Technology Development & Utilization Programme for Women (TDUPW), assistance is provided to projects relevant to technology development and utilization by women.

Objectives

  • Promoting the adoption of new technologies by women.
  • Creating awareness and providing training to women on technology-related issues.
  • Promoting technological upgradation of tiny, small and medium enterprises run by women entrepreneurs.
  • Showcasing appropriate technologies and organizing demonstration programmes for the benefit of women.
  • Design and development of products and processes beneficial to women.

Benefits

Duration of the Project

Projects should be time-bound depending on their nature. Normally, a duration of less than two years is preferred.

Quantum of Financial Assistance
  • Assistance is provided for projects relevant to technology development and utilization by women.
  • Assistance may be financial (partial or full) and/or technical guidance.
  • Financial assistance is provided only for expenditures on manpower, consumables, travel within the country and other miscellaneous items.

Note: The type of assistance required should be clearly mentioned in the proposal. No support is provided for basic infrastructure and buildings.

Method of Release of Financial Assistance
  • Assistance is released in instalments linked to milestones achieved as per the project schedule.
  • Release is subject to submission of progress reports and utilization certificates.

Eligibility

For Projects
  • Studies and surveys assessing technology-related information needs of women.
  • Documentation and content development on:
    • Technologies useful for production activities, personal care and community management.
    • Best practices in the use of technology for strengthening women’s gainful activities.
    • Contributions of women innovators and entrepreneurs.
    • Contributions of women scientists and technologists.
    • Technologies and products beneficial to women.
  • Establishment of consultancy cells for imparting technical knowledge on latest technologies.
  • Awareness creation and training of women in useful technologies such as food processing, water conservation, waste disposal and health management.
  • Case studies of successful R&D, technology development and businesswomen.
For Institutions
  • Government and non-government organizations and professional bodies working in technology development for women.
  • Agencies involved in promotion of S&T, R&D, entrepreneurship development and SME promotion.
  • Eligible organizations include:
    • Institutions receiving grants from Central/State Government agencies.
    • Institutions registered under the Societies Registration Act.
    • Institutions incorporated under the Companies Act.
    • Professional and industry associations.
    • Trusts registered under the Indian Trusts Act.

Documents Required

  1. Forwarding letter of the project proposal and undertaking from the head of the institution.
  2. Certificate of responsibility and commitment from investigators.
  3. Technical and financial details of the proposal.
  4. For non-government institutions:
    • Copy of Registration Certificate.
    • Copy of Annual Report for the last 3 years.
    • Copy of Auditor’s Report for the last 3 years.
