National Scheme of Incentive to Girls for Secondary Education

Women & Child

Details

The National Scheme of Incentive to Girls for Secondary Education promotes the girl child's enrolment in the age group of 14–18 years at the secondary stage, who passes class 8th and subsequently drops out for various socio-economic reasons. The scheme aims to retain such girl children up to class 12th. In the year 2004–05, the dropout rate of girls from classes 1st to 8th was about 50.8% and for classes 1st to 10th was about 64%. Hence, only 36% of the country’s girl students could be retained up to class 10th. A major contributor to this issue is the inability of parents to afford the cost of education of the girl child.

Benefits

  • A sum of ₹3000/- (Rupees three thousand only) is deposited as a term/fixed deposit in a public sector bank or post office in the name of the eligible girl child.
  • The deposit period is counted from the date of deposit until the girl child attains the age of 18 years.
  • No premature withdrawal is allowed.

Eligibility

  • The applicant should be a girl child.
  • The applicant should belong to Scheduled Caste (SC) or Scheduled Tribe (ST).
  • The applicant should have passed class 8th from Kasturba Gandhi Balika Vidyalayas (irrespective of caste).
  • The applicant should have enrolled in class 9th in State/UT Government, Government-aided, or local body schools from the academic year 2008–09 onwards.

Exclusions

  • Married girls are excluded from the scheme.
  • Girl children studying in private unaided schools are excluded.
  • Students enrolled in schools run by the Central Government are excluded.

Documents Required

  1. Scanned copy of the applicant’s Aadhaar card.
  2. Domicile certificate.
  3. Income certificate of parents or legal guardian issued by the respective state or UT authority.
  4. Class 10th scorecard of the applicant.
  5. Certificate from the school head/principal mentioning two-year continuation after enrolment in Class 9th.
  6. Fee receipts of the current class.
  7. Bank account proof of the applicant (existing account or joint account with a parent).
  8. Disability certificate (if applicable).
