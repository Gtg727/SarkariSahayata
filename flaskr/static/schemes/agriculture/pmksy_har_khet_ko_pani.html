Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana: Har Khet Ko Pani

Agriculture | Irrigation | Water Resources | Farmers

Details

The “Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana: Har Khet Ko Pani” (PMKSY–HKKP) is a major component of PMKSY launched by the Ministry of Jal Shakti, Government of India. It aims to expand cultivable land under assured irrigation, improve water use efficiency, and ensure that every farm receives adequate water for irrigation.

Objectives

  • Provide assured irrigation to every farm in the country (“Har Khet Ko Pani”).
  • Accelerate the implementation of ongoing irrigation projects.
  • Enhance the efficiency of water conveyance and distribution systems.
  • Promote groundwater development and command area development (CAD).
  • Encourage participatory irrigation management (PIM) through water user associations.

Key Components

  • Command Area Development (CAD): Improve the efficiency of existing irrigation systems.
  • Surface Minor Irrigation (SMI): Develop small irrigation projects using surface water sources.
  • Repair, Renovation & Restoration (RRR): Restore traditional water bodies and tanks.
  • Ground Water Development (GWD): Develop groundwater potential in water-deficient areas.

Benefits

  • Financial assistance for construction, restoration, and maintenance of irrigation systems.
  • Improved access to water for small and marginal farmers.
  • Better crop productivity and income stability through assured irrigation.
  • Enhanced water conservation and sustainable groundwater use.

Eligibility

  • All farmers with cultivable land are eligible to benefit from irrigation facilities created under the scheme.
  • Priority is given to small and marginal farmers in water-scarce and drought-prone areas.
  • Projects are implemented through state irrigation or agriculture departments.

Application Process

  1. Farmers may approach their local Agriculture or Irrigation Department office for inclusion in the irrigation project.
  2. Submit the necessary land and identity documents as required.
  3. Upon approval, farmers receive access to irrigation facilities developed under the scheme.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar Card
  • Land Ownership Proof
  • Bank Account Details
  • Address Proof
  • Passport Size Photograph
