Details

Resultant to this historic amendment, bamboo grown in non-forest areas is out of the scope of forest produce regulations. During the year 2022-23, the National Bamboo Mission (NBM) has been merged with the Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture (MIDH) scheme.

The restructured NBM primarily focuses on the development of the complete value chain of the bamboo sector — linking growers with consumers, starting from planting material, plantation, and creation of facilities, to skilled manpower, brand processing, marketing, and MSME development. The scheme is currently implemented in 24 States and UTs. It also supports setting up units for bio-energy extraction, activated carbon, charcoal making, pellet making, ethanol gasifier, etc., for both government and private entrepreneurs.

NBM also aims to revitalize the bamboo industry through innovations and policy support that help reduce carbon emissions and contribute to domestic manufacturing.

Key Objectives:

  • To increase bamboo plantation in non-forest government and private lands to supplement farm income, enhance resilience to climate change, and provide quality raw materials for industries.
  • To improve post-harvest management through establishment of innovative primary processing units, treatment and seasoning plants, preservation technologies, and market infrastructure.
  • To promote product development based on market demand by assisting R&D, entrepreneurship, and MSMEs to feed larger industries.
  • To rejuvenate the underdeveloped bamboo industry in India.
  • To promote skill development, capacity building, and awareness for overall development of the bamboo sector from production to marketing.
  • To reduce dependency on imports of bamboo and bamboo products by improving productivity and promoting domestic raw material use.

Benefits

  • Assists farmers, government agencies, artisans, entrepreneurs, private organizations, SHGs, FPOs, and other stakeholders in the bamboo industry.
  • Supports establishment of bamboo nurseries, cultivation, post-harvest processing, product manufacturing, and entrepreneurship development.
  • Encourages self-reliance to meet the national demand for bamboo and bamboo-based products.

Eligibility

The scheme is applicable to all farmers in India.

Application Process

Online

Applicants should refer to their respective State’s Bamboo Mission Portal for the online application link.

  1. Apply to the State Bamboo Mission (SBM) through the official state portal or offline submission.
  2. Scrutiny and approval of the proposal by the SBM.
  3. Credit-linked financing through scheduled/commercial banks.
  4. Progress reporting and project completion.
  5. Evaluation by SBM and disbursal of subsidy to the beneficiary’s bank account.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar number
  • Land documents
  • Caste Certificate (SC/ST only)
  • Phone details
  • Bank details
  • Photographs
  • DPR (Detailed Project Report)

The specific documents required may vary depending on the type of intervention and the state. Beneficiaries should consult relevant authorities for detailed information.
