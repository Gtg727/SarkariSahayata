National Food Security Mission (NFSM)

Agriculture | Food Production | Farmers | Sustainability

Details

The National Food Security Mission (NFSM) was launched in 2007 by the Government of India to increase the production of rice, wheat, pulses, and coarse cereals through area expansion and productivity enhancement. It aims to ensure food security for all citizens while promoting sustainable agricultural growth.

Objectives

  • Increase production of rice, wheat, pulses, and coarse cereals through productivity enhancement.
  • Ensure food security and reduce dependence on imports.
  • Promote improved technologies and farm mechanization.
  • Encourage sustainable farming practices to maintain soil health.

Key Components

  • NFSM-Rice: Support for hybrid seed production, soil health improvement, and integrated pest management.
  • NFSM-Wheat: Financial assistance for certified seeds, demonstrations, and efficient irrigation.
  • NFSM-Pulses: Promotion of pulse cultivation through improved varieties and cluster demonstrations.
  • NFSM-Coarse Cereals: Support for millets and other nutritious crops to improve food diversity.

Benefits

  • Financial assistance for seeds, fertilizers, and farm machinery.
  • Improved productivity and profitability for farmers.
  • Capacity building through training and demonstrations.
  • Encouragement for sustainable and eco-friendly farming.

Eligibility

  • All farmers cultivating rice, wheat, pulses, or coarse cereals.
  • Priority given to small and marginal farmers.
  • Farmers must be registered with their local Agriculture Department.

Application Process

  1. Visit your nearest Agriculture Department office or the official NFSM portal.
  2. Fill in the application form with crop and land details.
  3. Attach required documents and bank account information.
  4. Receive approval and financial assistance directly in your account.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar Card
  • Bank Account Details
  • Land Ownership Proof
  • Crop Details
  • Passport Size Photograph
