Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana: Per Drop More Crop

About the Scheme

The scheme “Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana: Per Drop More Crop” was launched by the Dept. of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare, Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare, Govt. of India on 1st July 2015. It mainly focuses on enhancing water use efficiency at the farm level through Micro Irrigation (Drip and Sprinkler Irrigation System). It also supports micro-level water storage and water conservation/management activities to supplement source creation for Micro Irrigation.

Objectives

  • Increase the area under micro irrigation technologies to enhance water use efficiency in the country.
  • Increase the productivity of crops and income of farmers through precision water management.
  • Promote micro-irrigation technologies in water-intensive crops like sugarcane, banana, and cotton.
  • Promote fertigation and integration of micro irrigation systems with solar energy.
  • Ensure efficient delivery and application of water at every farm.
  • Create employment opportunities for skilled and unskilled persons in installation and maintenance.

Key Features

PMKSY not only focuses on creating water sources for assured irrigation but also ensures protective irrigation by harnessing rainwater at the micro level through ‘Jal Sanchay’ and ‘Jal Sinchan’. Micro irrigation is an integral component to maximize water use efficiency.

  • Four components: AIBP, PDMC, Har Khet Ko Pani, and Watershed Development.
  • Per Drop More Crop focuses on micro-level storage structures, efficient conveyance, and precision irrigation systems.
  • Supports integration of solar energy for pressurized irrigation.
  • Implemented by the Department of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare (DAC&FW).

Benefits

  • Financial assistance for installation of Micro Irrigation systems.
  • Direct Benefit Transfer into farmers’ bank accounts.
  • 55% subsidy for small/marginal farmers and 45% for others.
  • Union Territories receive 100% central funding.
  • Support for water harvesting, lifting devices, and farm ponds.

Eligibility

  • The applicant must be an Indian citizen.
  • All farmers across States and Union Territories are eligible.
  • Subsidy benefits limited to 5 hectares per beneficiary.
  • Only BIS-marked systems/components are eligible.
  • Aadhaar details required for DBT benefit access.

Application Process (Offline)

  1. Contact your Block/District Agriculture Office through your Gram Panchayat.
  2. Collect and fill out the application form for the scheme.
  3. Attach self-attested mandatory documents and a signed passport-size photograph.
  4. Submit the filled form to the designated receiving authority.
  5. Collect the acknowledgment receipt upon submission.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar Card
  • Bank Account Details
  • Address Proof
  • Passport-size Photograph
  • Caste Certificate (if applicable)
  • Proof of Agriculture Land Ownership
  • Domicile Certificate of State/UT
