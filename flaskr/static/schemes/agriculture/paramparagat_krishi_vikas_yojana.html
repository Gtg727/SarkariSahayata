Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)

Agriculture | Organic Farming | Sustainability | Farmers

Details

Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) was launched by the Department of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, Ministry of Agriculture, Government of India in 2015 to promote organic farming across the country. It encourages farmers to adopt traditional and sustainable agricultural practices using organic inputs.

Objectives

  • Promote organic farming and reduce the dependence on chemical fertilizers and pesticides.
  • Improve soil health and fertility through natural farming practices.
  • Ensure sustainable and eco-friendly agricultural production.
  • Promote certification and branding of organic products for better marketing.
  • Empower farmers through training, certification, and direct market access.

Key Components

  • Cluster Approach: Farmers are organized into clusters for collective organic farming on 20 hectares of land.
  • Organic Inputs Support: Financial assistance for bio-fertilizers, vermicomposting, and natural pest control.
  • Certification: Support for organic certification under the Participatory Guarantee System (PGS).
  • Marketing and Branding: Promoting organic produce through branding and marketing linkages.

Benefits

  • Financial assistance of ₹50,000 per hectare for three years to farmers for organic conversion.
  • Improved soil health and increased long-term productivity.
  • Access to organic certification and marketing channels.
  • Reduction in cost of cultivation due to use of on-farm organic inputs.

Eligibility

  • All Indian farmers willing to adopt organic farming practices.
  • Farmers must form a cluster group consisting of at least 50 farmers covering 50 acres.
  • Priority is given to small and marginal farmers.

Application Process

  1. Farmers can apply through their respective State Agriculture Department.
  2. Form a cluster group with nearby farmers interested in organic farming.
  3. Submit required documents and bank details for financial assistance.
  4. Approved clusters receive support for training, input costs, and certification.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar Card
  • Bank Account Details
  • Address Proof
  • Land Ownership Proof
  • Passport Size Photograph
  • Cluster Group Details (if applicable)
