Central Assistance for Displaced Families from PoK & Chhamb

Rehabilitation | Financial Assistance | Displaced Families

Details

The Central Assistance Scheme for displaced families from Pakistan Occupied Kashmir (PoK) and Chhamb areas was launched by the Government of India to provide financial support and rehabilitation measures for those displaced due to the 1947–48, 1965, and 1971 Indo-Pak conflicts. The scheme aims to ensure proper resettlement and social integration of these families.

Objectives

  • Provide financial relief to displaced families from PoK and Chhamb.
  • Support permanent rehabilitation through direct benefit transfer.
  • Ensure equitable distribution of funds and transparency in implementation.

Benefits

  • One-time financial assistance of ₹5.5 lakh per family through DBT.
  • Support for housing, land development, and livelihood generation.
  • Direct transfer to beneficiaries’ bank accounts.

Eligibility

  • Families displaced from PoK (1947–48) and Chhamb (1965 & 1971) areas.
  • Beneficiaries identified by the Government of Jammu & Kashmir under verified lists.
  • Applicants must have valid identity proof and be residents of India.

Application Process

Eligible families can apply through the designated District Administration office or the official J&K Rehabilitation Department portal. Applications must include valid documents proving displacement status and family details.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar Card / Identity Proof
  • Displacement Certificate / Verification from District Authority
  • Bank Account Details (for DBT)
  • Address Proof and Family Details
